Hydrodynamics

Term Description
Streamline flow In such a flow of liquid in a tube each particle follows the path of its preceding particle.
Turbulent flow It is irregular flow which does not obey above condition
Berrnoulli's principle p + 1 2 ρu2 + ρgh = constant
Continuity of flow A1v1 = A2v2 where A1 and A2 are the area of cross section of tube of variable cross section and v1 and v2are the velocity of flow of liquids crossing these areas
Viscosity Viscous force between two layers of fluid of area A and velocity gradient dv/dx is F = ηA dv dx where η is the coefficient of viscosity
Stokes law Viscous force on a spherical body of radius r falling through a liquid of viscosity η is F = 6πηrv where v is velocity of the sphere

Hydostatics

Term Description
Fluid pressure It is force exerted normally on a unit area of surface of fluid P = F A Its unit is Pascal 1Pa = 1Nm-2
Pascal's Law Pressure in a fluid in equilibriumis same everywhere
Density Density of a substance is defined as the mass per unit volume.
Atompheric Pressure Weight of all the air above the earth causes atmospheric pressure which exerts pressure on the surface of earth.Atmospheric pressure at sea level is P0 = 1.01 x 10-5Pa
Guage pressure P = P0 + ρgh,pressure at any point in fluid is sum of atmospheric pressure and pressure due to all the fluid above that point
Archimedes principle When a solid body is fully or partly immersed in a fluid it experiences a buoyant force equal to the weight of fluid displaced by it.
Upthrust It is the weight of the displaced liquid
Boyle's law PV = constant
Charle's law V T = constant
