Concept of relative motion:

For two object moving with velocity Va and Vb,relative velocity is defined as Vba=Va-Vb and vice-versa.

Case Description
For straight line motion If the object are moving in same direction,relative velocity can be get by substracting other. If they are moving in opposite direction, relative velocity will be get by adding the velocities.
For two dimension motion if
Va=Vxai+Vyaj and Vb=Vxbi+Vybj then relative velocity of B to A
=i(Vxb-Vxa)+j(Vyb-Vya)
For three dimension motion Va=Vxai+Vyaj+Vzaz
Vb=Vxbi+Vybj+Vzbz
Now,the relative velocity between the two
=i(Vxb-Vxa)+j(Vyb-Vya)+z(Vzb-Vza)

Free fall accleration:

Freely falling motion of any body under the effect of gravity is an example of uniformly accelerated motion.

Kinematics equation of motion can under gravity can be found out using g in place of a.

Therfore,Kinematics motion under gravity are:
V=Vo+gt
S=Vot+12(gt2)
v2=(Vo)2+2gx
Value of g is equal to 9.8m.s-2. The value of g is taken positive when the body falls vertically downwards and negative when the body is projected upwards.

Friction:

What is friction ?

Friction is a force which act between bodies whenever there is a relative motion between them. When bodies slip fictional force is called static and when and when they do not slip its called kinetic frictional force.

Kinetic frictional force:

When bodies slip over each other f=μkN. Where N is the normal contact over the surface and μk is the coefficient of kinetic friction. Direction of friction is such that the relative slipping is opposed by the friction.

Static frictional force:

Frictional force can also act is there is no relative motion. Such force is called static frctional force. Maximum static force that a body can exert on other body in contact with it is called limiting friction.
f=μsN Where, N is the normal contact force between the surface and μs is the coefficient of static friction. Fmax is the maximum possible force of static friction.
Note that μs>μk and angle of friction is tan λ=μs

Frame of refrence:

Frame of refrence:

Inertial frame of refrence is those attached to objects which are at rest or moving at constant velocity. Newton's law are valid for inertial frame of refrence . Example:
Person standing in train moving with a constant velocity

Non inertial frame of refrence:

Inertial frame of refrence is attached to acclerated object for example:
A person standing in a train moving with an increasing speed. To apply Newton's law, pseudo force have to be introduced in the equation whose value will be F=-ma.
