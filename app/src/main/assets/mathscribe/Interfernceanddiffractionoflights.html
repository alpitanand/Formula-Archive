Huygen's Principle and Interference of Light

Term Description
Wave front It is the locus of points in the medium which at any instant are vibrating in the same phase
Huygen's Principle
1.) Each point on the given primary Wavefront acts as a source of secondary wavelets spreading out disturbance in all direction
2.) The tangential plane to these secondary wavelets constitutes the new wave front.
Interference It is the phenomenon of non uniform distribution of energy in the medium due to superposition of two light waves
Condition of maximum intensity Φ = 2nπ or x = nλ where n=0,1,2,3.....
Condition of minimum intensity Φ = 2(n+1)π or x = (2n+1)λ/2 where n=0,1,2,3.....
Ratio of maximum and minimum intensity Imax Imin = (a1 + a2)2 (a1 - a2)2
Distance of nth bright gringe from centre of the screen yn = nDλ d where d is the seperation distance between two coherent source of light, D is the distance screen and slit, λ is the wavelength of light used.
Angular position of nth bright fringe θn = yn D = nλ d
Distance of nth dark fringe from centre of the screen yn' = (2n + 1)Dλ 2d
Angular position of nth dark fringe θn' = yn' D = (2n + 1)λ 2d
Fringe width β = Dλ d

Polarisation It is the phenomenon due to which vibrations of light are restricted in a particular plane.
Brewster's law μ = tanp where μ is refractive index of medium and p is angle of polarisation
