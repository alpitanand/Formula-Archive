Mechanics of system of particles

Term Description
Centre of mass It is that point where the entire mass of the system is imagined to be concentrated ,for consideration of its translational motion
Poistion vector of centre of mass Rcm=Σ riMi ΣMi where ri is the coordinate of element i and Mi is mass of element i
In coordinate system Xcm= Σ xiMi ΣMi
Ycm= Σ yiMi ΣMi
Zcm= Σ ziMi ΣMi
Velocity of CM VCM= Σ viMi ΣMi
The total momentum of a system of particles is equal the total mass times the velocity of the centre of mass
Force When Newton's second law of motion is applied to the system of particles we find Ftα=MaCM with aCM=d2RCM/dt2
Thus centre of mass of the system mves as if all the mass of the system were concentrated at the centre of mass and external force were applied to that point.
Momentum conservation in COM motionP=MvCM which means that total linear momentum of system of particles is equal to the product of total mass of the system and the velocity of its centre of mass

Rigid body dynamics

Term Description
Angular Displacement -When a rigid body rotates about a fixed axis, the angular displacement is the angle Δϴ swept out by a line passing through any point on the body and intersecting the axis of rotation perpendicularly
-It can be positive(counter clockwise) or negative(clockwise).
Analogous to a component of the displacement vector.
-SI Unit: radian(rad). other units:degree,revolution
Angular velocity -Average angular velocity, is equal to Δϴ/Δt.
-Instantaneous Angular Velocity ω=dϴ/dt.
-Angular velocity can be positive or negative

Elasticity

Term Description
Elasticity The ability of a body to regain its original shape and size when deforming force is withdrawn
Stress Stress= F A where F is applied force and A is area over which it acts.
Strain It is the ratio of the change in size or shape to the original size or shape.
Longitudinal strain= ΔI I
Volume strain= ΔV V
and shear strain is due to change in shape of the body
Hook's law Hook's law is the fundamental law of elasticity and is stated as "for small deformations stress is proportional to strain"
stress strain =constant
This constant is knows as modulas of elasticity of a given material
Elastic Modulus
Young's Modulus of elasticity Y = FI AΔI
Bulk Modulus of Elasticity K = - VΔP ΔV
Modulus of Rigidity η = F Aθ
Poisson's Ratio The ratio of lateral strain to the longitudinal strain is called Poisson's ratio which is constant for material of that body.
σ = IΔD DΔI
Strain energy Energy stored per unit volume is a strained wire is E = 1/2(stress)x(strain)
